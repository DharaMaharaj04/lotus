Lotus Temple

A Marvel of Architecture and Spiritual Harmony

Basic Facts

  • Location: Bahapur, New Delhi
  • Faith: Bahá'í House of Worship
  • Completed: 1986
  • Architect: Fariborz Sahba (Iranian)
  • Built by: Larsen & Toubro
  • Construction Time: ~6 years (1979–1986)

Design Inspired by the Lotus

  • Symbolism: Lotus symbolizes purity, peace, love, and immortality
  • 27 marble petals form 9-sided structure
  • Central Hall: 34.27 m high, 70 m diameter
  • Accommodates up to 2,500 people
  • No pulpits, idols, or pictures — symbol of unity

Structural Engineering Marvel

  • Material: White Pentelic marble (also used in the Parthenon)
  • Framework: Reinforced concrete covered in marble
  • Petals use ribbed shell structure for strength and elegance
  • No external lighting — reflects moonlight
  • Daytime illumination via natural light — energy-efficient

Philosophical & Functional Principles

  • Open to people of all faiths
  • No rituals, sermons, or sectarian symbols
  • Focus on unity, meditation, and harmony
  • Surrounded by 9 reflecting pools and lush gardens
